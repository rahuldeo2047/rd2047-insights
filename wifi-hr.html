Act 1  ·  The Concept

What is WiFi-HR-Est?

Contactless heart rate and respiration rate estimation from WiFi Channel State Information (CSI). ESP32-S3 receivers capture how the WiFi channel changes as a human body moves, breathes, and pulses. Tiny chest-wall displacements from heartbeat and breathing modulate the multipath reflections — CSI captures those modulations.


Pivoted from camera-based approach for privacy. A camera in a bedroom or bathroom has a higher privacy cost than a WiFi receiver already present in the environment. The ESP32-S3 is a commodity device. No frame capture, no image data, no biometric inference from visual content. Just RF phase and amplitude fluctuations.

Physics of CSI-HR
WiFi CSI measures complex-valued channel response across subcarriers — amplitude and phase per carrier. Heartbeat chest-wall displacement is ~0.5–1mm. Breathing displacement is ~5–10mm. Both modulate the received signal's phase. The bandpass filter separates them: 0.15–0.4Hz respiration, 0.8–2Hz cardiac. The technique works; the question is how well it works in real environments with motion interference.
Current state in one sentence
The pipeline runs end-to-end and outputs a BPM number. Whether that BPM number is correct is unknown — no ECG, PPG, or chest-strap has been placed next to the system to check.

DSP pipeline — complete

Full signal processing chain. All stages implemented. Parameters listed are current values — not theoretical optima.

Pipeline stages

# ── Bandpass filter ───────────────────────────────────

# ── FFT ───────────────────────────────────────────────

# ── Kalman filter ─────────────────────────────────────

KALMAN_MEAS_VAR = 1.0

# ── Presence detection ────────────────────────────────

⚠️ presence detection unreliable at this threshold
Espnow variant note
The espnow variant operates at 20Hz packet rate. Nyquist for 2Hz cardiac upper bound requires >4Hz minimum — 20Hz provides only 10x margin. This is technically sufficient but leaves little headroom for artifact rejection. The standard variant with auto-subcarrier selection at 100Hz is preferred for accuracy.
Act 3  ·  Known Issues

Honest inventory — what does not work

The pipeline compiles, runs, and produces output. That output has not been validated. These are not future risks — they are current gaps in the build.

❌
No ground truth validation
No ECG, PPG, or chest-strap has been placed next to the system. BPM output exists; accuracy unknown.
❌
Zero subject-count study
Single operator, indoor, static. No multi-subject test. No Bland-Altman analysis. No confidence interval.
❌
Presence detection brittle
Variance threshold 1e-7 produces false positives in quiet rooms and false negatives with motion. No adaptive threshold implemented.
⚠️
Espnow: 20Hz rate
Nyquist margin for 2Hz cardiac signal is 10x. Technically valid. Leaves no room for artifact windowing.
⚠️
Fixed subcarrier 30
Subcarrier 30 selected empirically, not adaptive. Best subcarrier varies by environment and subject position. Auto-select (subcarrier 28 logic) not yet robust.
✅
Pipeline runs end-to-end
CSI → DSP → Kalman → BPM output. No runtime errors. Output is plausible (40–160 BPM range). Accuracy unknown.
Act 4  ·  Roadmap

Validation roadmap

The DSP problem is solved. The validation problem is not started. These four steps convert the pipeline from "outputs a number" to "produces a defensible measurement."

STEP 1
PPG / chest-strap ground truth
Run WiFi-HR alongside a Polar H10 or equivalent. Log simultaneous time-series. First data point on accuracy.
STEP 2
Multi-subject Bland-Altman
N ≥ 10 subjects. Bland-Altman plot for HR and RR. Report limits of agreement with 95% CI. This is the minimum bar for any accuracy claim.
STEP 3
Motion / posture characterization
Systematic tests: sitting still, talking, typing, minor movement, standing. Map failure envelope. Know when the system is unreliable.
STEP 4
HRV-EEG coherence
Cross-fuse WiFi-HR HRV output with S.H.R.A.V.Y.A EEG alpha coherence. Physiological relationship is well-established; measuring it at home is the novel contribution.
Literature context
CSI-based HR estimation has published precedent: Adib et al. (MIT 2015 Vital-Radio), Wang et al. (2017 E-eyes), multiple 2020–2024 papers on ESP32 CSI-HR. Reported MAE in controlled conditions: 1–5 BPM. In uncontrolled/moving environments: 5–15 BPM. This system makes no accuracy claim until Bland-Altman study complete.
