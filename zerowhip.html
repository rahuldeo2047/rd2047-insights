Act 1  ·  The Problem

What is ZeroWhip?

Shoulder-mounted tendon-driven robotic arm that holds a phone at eye level. The engineering thesis: tech neck is a posture problem caused by hardware — phone held below eye line, head bent forward, sustained load on cervical vertebrae. Fix the hardware, fix the posture.


String-driven rigid joints. Strings transmit tension from servo motors through a cable routing system to actuate each arm segment. Codename: ZeroWhip. Deployment name: NeckGuardian. The name reflects the clinical objective — neck protection, not just convenience.

Why tendon-driven?
Tendon routing moves the actuator mass (servos) to the shoulder mount, keeping the distal arm lightweight. A direct-drive arm of comparable reach would impose unacceptable torque on the shoulder joint. String-driven rigid joints solve both the weight distribution and the actuator placement problem simultaneously.
Sister projects
S.H.R.A.V.Y.A (DIY EEG) cross-validates posture-correlated EEG signals. WiFi-HR-Est and Visual-HR are downstream biometric validation layers. ZeroWhip is the physical intervention; the EEG system measures whether it works neurologically.

YOLO latency context
220–350ms YOLO inference feeds into the deadband filter (10°) then servo command. Total chain latency: approximately 2.5 servo update cycles. Acceptable for slow intentional phone repositioning. Not acceptable for reactive posture correction. The arm currently tracks, it does not anticipate.
Act 3  ·  Failure Modes

Known failure modes — honest inventory

Build logs rarely list failures. This one does. Every item below is observed on the physical hardware, not hypothetical.

  • ❌
    Post-idle oscillation
    ~80ms shake when arm resumes from idle. Root cause: integral windup not fully compensated by anti-windup ±20 clamp. The integrator accumulates error during idle; when command resumes, the accumulated integral overshoots before the derivative term catches it. Will check it later — commit 5afd033.
  • ❌
    End-effector accuracy unmeasured
    No ground-truth measurement exists for where the phone actually lands vs. where IK commands it to go. The arm moves, phone ends up somewhere near eye level. The error budget is unknown. This is Phase 2a's most critical gap.
  • ⚠️
    Servo latency chain
    YOLO 220–350ms → deadband 10° → servo command. Total: ~2.5 servo update cycles at 100Hz. The chain is long enough to introduce perceptible lag at fast head movements. Acceptable for the current use case (phone held stationary) — becomes a problem if the arm needs to track head movement dynamically.
  • ⚠️
    Workspace singularities
    IK protects against singularities via 95% reach scaling (never commands to full 0.45m extent) + 5mm dead-zone around origin. This avoids divide-by-zero in the IK solver but means ~5% of the theoretical workspace is unreachable by design. Not a problem in practice — the usable envelope is sufficient for phone positioning.
