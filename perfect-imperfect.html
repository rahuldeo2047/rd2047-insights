The Book

Hard science, mythic weight

Perfect Imperfect is a completed hard sci-fi novel that braids real physics with older stories — resilience and women's power at its centre, Krishna at its edges, and time travel built on quantum mechanics that tries to stay honest to the equations. It asks what a person owes to a future they can see and a past they cannot change.

women & resilience Krishna time travel quantum physics hard science myth × manifold

"The same equations that let a clock disagree with itself let a story do the same."

The premise, the turns, and the ending stay off this page — that is what beta readers are for. What is public is the shape: a novel that takes both its science and its mythology seriously, and refuses to dumb down either.

Where It Is

Complete. Unpublished. On purpose.

The manuscript is finished. Before it goes to literary agents, it needs a small circle of honest readers — the kind who will say what does not work, not what is polite. The path is deliberate: beta readers → revision → agent query (hard sci-fi representation).

What I'm looking for
2–3 serious beta readers who read hard sci-fi (Greene, Liu Cixin, Egan, Banks) and who will mark the page where they got bored, confused, or stopped believing. Honesty > encouragement. You get an early full read; I get a sharper book.
Read it before the agents do
If you read hard sci-fi and want a full early copy in exchange for honest notes, write to the author. Tell me one novel you loved and why — that's the whole application.
