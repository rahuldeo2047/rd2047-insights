Act 0  ·  The Provocation

An ordinary rock holds a philosophical bomb

General relativity predicts — and experiment confirms — that the top of a rock ages faster than its base. Over a single year, the top of a 1.7-metre object accumulates roughly 5.8 nanoseconds more real, physical time than its bottom. This is not a measurement illusion or a coordinate artifact. It is a frame-independent, coordinate-invariant scalar fact.


So here is the question the paper turns on: what does it take for the rock to hold together across that temporal gap? The head and the feet are living in slightly different amounts of time. Something has to bind them — and what that something requires of spacetime is where things get strange.

Why the title overstates on purpose
The form deliberately echoes "Attention Is All You Need" — short, declarative, built to provoke argument rather than hedge into being ignored. The argument inside is explicitly conditional. That is stated as a feature, not a weakness.

The chain of established facts
The paper does not introduce new physics. It connects three independently confirmed results: a gravitational gradient exists across any macroscopic body; electromagnetic binding requires a well-defined causal past; and a well-defined causal structure implies a specific global geometry. The novelty is the connection — not the ingredients.
Act 2  ·  The Vocabulary

Five ideas, in plain language

No equations required to follow the question. Five terms carry the argument.

Proper time (τ)
The time a physical clock actually measures along its path. A scalar invariant — the same in every frame. Two clocks at different heights accumulate different proper times even if they start and finish at the same place.
Worldtube
The four-dimensional volume a finite-size object sweeps out as it moves through spacetime — the "shadow" a 3D object casts in 4D.
Block universe (eternalism)
Past, present and future all exist equally as parts of one fixed four-dimensional structure. "Now" is a location in it, not a moving boundary.
Growing-block universe
The past and present are real; the future does not yet exist. A "now" boundary divides the real from the not-yet-real.
Presentism
Only the present moment is real. Past and future do not exist.
Act 3  ·  The Novelty

Not the old argument in new clothes

The 1960s Rietdijk–Putnam argument also reached the block universe — but through the relativity of simultaneity, which leaves it open to the conventionality-of-simultaneity objection. This argument takes a different road, using only frame-invariant quantities throughout.

The one-line claim
For a bound physical object there is no single frame-invariant "now" — the head and the feet disagree, and neither is wrong. The paper argues that the cohesion of bound matter constrains what the ontology of time can be. How and why is the part under peer review.
