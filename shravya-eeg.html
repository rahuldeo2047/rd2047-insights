Act 1  ·  The Problem

Hypertension without a cuff

Hypertension affects 1.3 billion people worldwide. Standard cuff-based measurement is episodic — you only know your BP when you measure it. EEG-based correlates offer a continuous, non-invasive alternative signal. Not a replacement for clinical BP measurement; a supplementary continuous marker.


Two EEG signatures correlate with elevated blood pressure in published literature: frontal alpha asymmetry (Fp1–Fp2 power ratio) and elevated delta/alpha ratio — both linked to stress and autonomic dysregulation. The hypothesis: measure these continuously from a wearable EEG headband, flag sustained anomalies, correlate against periodic cuff readings.

Target markers
Frontal alpha asymmetry: Fp1 vs Fp2 alpha power ratio. Reduced right-frontal alpha associated with negative affect and sympathetic activation — mechanisms linked to BP elevation.

Delta/alpha ratio: Elevated delta relative to alpha in frontal channels — marker of stress and autonomic dysregulation. Inversely correlated with relaxation-state BP readings in several studies.

Both markers require frontal channel pair (Fp1, Fp2) minimum. Two-electrode design sufficient for initial validation.
Honest scope
EEG correlates of hypertension are documented but not diagnostic. Correlation studies show statistical association, not reliable individual prediction. This device will be validated against BP cuff readings on a single subject before any broader claim is made. The goal at this stage: demonstrate correlation exists in operator data. Not a medical device. Not FDA-cleared. Not a cuff replacement.

ADS1299 vs ADS1263
ADS1299 was designed for biopotential measurement — 8 simultaneous differential channels, 24-bit, internal reference, 1µVpp input-referred noise. ADS1263 is a precision ADC not designed for EEG — mux-switched channels introduce crosstalk and settling delays. For hypertension markers (2 frontal channels), either chip is sufficient at single-channel. ADS1299 is preferred for future expansion. Decision pending chip availability and PCB cost.
Act 3  ·  Measurement Approach

EEG signal pipeline — planned

Two frontal electrodes (Fp1, Fp2) are sufficient for the hypertension markers targeted. Pipeline below is planned — not yet implemented. Chip installation is gate 0.

Planned acquisition pipeline
ADC — not installed → SPI bridge → BLE stream → RPi5 FFT → Alpha / Delta power → Asymmetry index
Sample rate target: 250 SPS (sufficient for alpha band 8–13Hz with margin). Bandpass: 0.5–40Hz notch-filtered at 50Hz (Indian mains). Alpha extraction: Welch PSD on 4-second windows, 50% overlap.
Alpha
8 – 13 Hz
5–100 µV scalp. Primary band for asymmetry index. Strongest at occipital — frontal amplitude lower (~10–30µV).
Delta
0.5 – 4 Hz
20–200 µV. Largest EEG band. Delta/alpha ratio = stress/autonomic marker. EMG artifact risk at this band.
Frontal Alpha Asymmetry
Fp1 – Fp2
log(right alpha) – log(left alpha). Negative value = right-frontal suppression = sympathetic dominance signal.
Noise floor target
ADC requirement
< 1µVpp input-referred. ADS1299 spec: 1µVpp. ADS1263 spec: ~1µV RMS at 10SPS — adequate for alpha/delta.
Act 4  ·  What's Blocked

Gate 0: install the chip

Everything downstream — signal acquisition, FFT, asymmetry index, BP correlation — is blocked on a single action: soldering the ADC chip onto the board. PCB layout exists. Chip not procured / placed.

Current blockers
1. ADC chip (ADS1263 or ADS1299) not yet purchased and installed.

2. Electrode procurement not started (Ag/AgCl snap electrodes, Fp1/Fp2 placement).

3. No signal acquisition code written yet.

4. Validation protocol not defined (cuff readings vs. EEG session timing, subject protocol, session length).
Validation plan (once chip installed)
Phase 1 — Single subject (operator): 10× sessions. Each session: 5-min rest EEG + BP cuff reading. Compute frontal alpha asymmetry + delta/alpha ratio. Correlate against cuff SBP/DBP.

Phase 2 — Multi-subject: 5+ subjects, same protocol. Bland-Altman analysis.

Claim threshold: r > 0.6 correlation on ≥ 10 sessions before any "works" statement is made publicly.
Act 5  ·  Sister Projects

Cross-validation orbit

WiFi-HR-Est
CSI-based contactless HR + HRV from ESP32-S3. HRV is a well-established autonomic marker correlated with BP. Once WiFi-HR is validated against PPG ground truth, it becomes a cross-check for the EEG hypertension markers — two independent autonomic signals, same subject, same session.
Visual-HR-Est
rPPG-based heart rate from RGB camera. Provides BVP waveform — another HRV source for triangulation. Magic-mirror form factor means both visual-HR and EEG could run simultaneously on the same hardware platform.
Long-term goal
Multi-modal hypertension monitor: EEG frontal alpha asymmetry + WiFi-HR HRV + rPPG BVP — all non-contact, privacy-first, running on commodity hardware. Each modality cross-validates the others. No single sensor needs clinical-grade accuracy if three independent signals agree.
